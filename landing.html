// THE PROBLEM

Every backtest you've ever run has lied to you

📸

Screenshots lie

A chart screenshot is a sample size of one — the winner you remembered. Nobody keeps score of the setups that quietly didn't work.

🎈

"It bounced" means nothing

Sure, price bounced after your level — but everything bounced in an up market. Did it bounce more than a random price? You never checked. Almost nobody does.

🎨

Backtests are decoration

TradingView and Excel let you tweak the rules on the same chart you're fitting to, until the equity curve looks beautiful. That's not a test.

🧠

Hindsight rigs the game

The result: you can't tell your real edges from your comforting stories. And you're risking money on both.

// FAQ

Questions, answered

What is TickerStrike?

A research platform that puts your trading ideas on trial — testing whether your setups carry a real, measurable edge against fair baselines and data they've never seen — so you can determine when and where is the right place to strike using a targeted plan with surgical precision.

Is this a signal service?

No. TickerStrike won't tell you what to buy this morning. It answers one question, well: does this belief have a measurable, trustworthy edge? What you do with the verdict is your call.

Is TickerStrike a broker? Does it place trades?

No. TickerStrike is a research and analysis tool. It tells you whether your setup is real — execution happens at your broker of choice.

Who is it for?

Retail traders who want to reality-check the setups they see on YouTube and Twitter before risking a dollar — skepticism as a service, in plain English. And quants who want a methodologically correct pre-registration, matched-null, out-of-sample harness without building it themselves.

What if my setup turns out to have no edge?

That's a win too. The engine assumes every idea is worthless and tries to kill it — most don't survive. Knowing which of your beliefs are myths is worth as much as confirming the real ones, because it's the myths that cost you money.

Is this investment advice?

No. TickerStrike tests your own research and ideas. Nothing on the platform constitutes investment advice, and trading involves risk.
